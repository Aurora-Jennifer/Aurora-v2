Aurora’s mathematical components are built on standard results from
statistics, optimization, machine learning, and quantitative finance.
Key contributors whose work underlies the equations include:

• Karl Pearson – z-score, standard deviation, correlation (1894)
• Charles Spearman – rank correlation IC (1904)
• Gauss & Legendre – least squares (1809)
• G.E.P. Box & Jenkins – exponential smoothing (1960s)
• J. L. Kelly Jr. – Kelly criterion (1956)
• Edward Thorp – Kelly in finance
• MacLean, Thorp & Ziemba – Kelly portfolio theory
• Harry Markowitz – Modern Portfolio Theory (1952)
• Hoerl & Kennard – ridge regularization (1970)
• Ledoit & Wolf – covariance shrinkage (2004)
• Thierry Roncalli – risk parity & convex risk budgeting (2013)
• Almgren & Chriss – market impact (2001)
• Albert Kyle – price impact model (1985)
• Robert Merton – timing risk (1969)
• López de Prado – triple barrier, meta-labeling, purged CV (2018)
• Auer, Cesa-Bianchi & Fischer – EXP3 (2002)
• Audibert & Bubeck – EXP3-IX (2009)
• Chuan Guo et al. – temperature scaling (2017)
• Hinton et al. – softmax temperature roots
• Leo Breiman – feature importance (2001)
• Friedman – boosting importance
• William Sharpe – Sharpe ratio (1966)
• Burke, Magdon-Ismail & Atiya – max drawdown analysis
• Claude Shannon – entropy (1948)
• Fisher – likelihood theory (1922)
• Cox – logistic likelihood (1958)
• Powers – AUC/F1 analysis (2011)
